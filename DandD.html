Race Description
Dragonborn Dragonborn look very similar to their namesake with the exception of the lack of wings or a tail. The first Dragonborn were said to be devout worshippers of Bahamut that were gifted with the characteristics of their deity. In any case, they are now a self sufficient race born from eggs. They have strong clan loyalties and are a proud, dignified race. They know the Common Tongue and Draconic languages.

Abilities
Breath Weapon: The character shoots a 25 foot line of elemental energy from their mouth, the type of which depending on the type of Dragonborn. The character also has damage resistance to their element. Choices include fire, lightning, acid, and frost. This ability is a primary action that can be used once but recharges after a long rest.
     Damage:
Dwarf Bold and hardy, dwarves are known as skilled warriors, miners, and workers of stone and metal. Though they stand well under 5 feet tall, dwarves are so broad and compact that they can weigh as much as a human standing nearly two feet taller. Their courage and endurance are also easily a match for any of the larger folk. Like the rock they live so closely with, they are stubborn and resistant to change. They know the Common Tongue and Dwarvish languages.

Abilities
Darkvison: Having spent much of their lives underground, Dwarves are able to see in little to no light.
Poison Resistance: Dwarves are hardy and gain a +2 bonus to Fortitude rolls against poison and disease.
Metalworking: Dwarves are very familiar with the mechanics of locks and simple mechanisms, allowing them to pick locks and set and disarm traps .
Elf Elves can live well over 700 years, giving them a broad perspective on events that might trouble the shorter-lived races more deeply. They are more often amused than excited, and more likely to be curious than greedy. They tend to remain aloof and unfazed by petty happenstance. When pursuing a goal, however, whether adventuring on a mission or learning a new skill or art, elves can be focused and relentless. They are slow to make friends and enemies, and even slower to forget them. Elves speak the Common Tongue and Elvish.

Abilities
Darkvison: Elves have spent many nights using just the moonlight that gets through the forest canopy to see, and as such can see in very dim lighting.
Trance: Elves don’t need to sleep. Instead, they meditate deeply, remaining semiconscious, for 4 hours a day. After resting in this way, they gain the same benefit that a human does from 8 hours of sleep.
Insightful: Elves gain a +1 bonus to perception.
Gnome A gnome’s energy and enthusiasm for living shines through every inch of his or her tiny body. Gnomes average slightly over 3 feet tall and weigh 40 to 45 pounds. Their tan or brown faces are usually adorned with broad smiles (beneath their prodigious noses), and their bright eyes shine with excitement. They are a very curious and impulsive race, though meticulous about things they deem important. Quick to laugh at jokes and at their own expense, gnomes are generally a kind-hearted people. Gnomes speak the Common Tongue plus one other language.

Abilities
Small Stature: Gnomes are considered a Small unit, giving them a +1 bonus to Sneak.
Boundless Energy: Gnomes have +1 max mana.
Halfling Halflings are an affable and cheerful people. The comforts of home are the goals of most halflings’ lives: a place to settle in peace and quiet, far from marauding monsters and clashing armies. Adventurers among them usually venture into the world for reasons of community, friendship, wanderlust, or curiosity rather than the desire for power and wealth that drives many other adventurers. They’re concerned with basic needs and simple pleasures and have little use for ostentation. They have a knack for finding the most straightforward solution to a problem, and have little patience for dithering. Though they are characterized as mild, they can display remarkable ferocity when their friends, families, or communities are threatened. Halflings know the Common Tongue.

Abilities
Small Stature: Halflings are considered a Small unit, giving them a +1 bonus to Sneak.
Lucky: If you roll a 1 using the d20, reroll it and take the new roll.
Wily: Halflings can move through squares occupied by Large size or larger units.
Half-Orc Whether united under the leadership of a mighty warlock or having fought to a standstill after years of conflict, orc and human tribes sometimes form alliances, joining forces into a larger horde to the terror of civilized lands nearby. As could be expected to happen during long periods of intermingling, half-orcs are born. Some half-orcs rise to become proud chiefs of orc tribes, their human blood giving them an edge over their full-blooded orc rivals. Some venture into the world to prove their worth among humans and other more civilized races. Half-orcs’ grayish pigmentation, sloping foreheads, jutting jaws, prominent teeth, and towering builds make their orcish heritage plain for all to see. Half-orcs stand between 5 and 7 feet tall and usually weigh between 180 and 250 pounds. Half-orcs are not evil by nature, but they feel the savagery of their orc heritage within them. They speak the Common Tongue and Orcish languages.

Abilities
Fearless: Half-orcs gain a +3 bonus to Will saves against being Frightened.
Aggressive: Half-orcs gain a +3 bonus to Charisma checks being used to intimidate.
Human Humans are the youngest of the common races, late to arrive on the world scene and short-lived in comparison to the other races. Perhaps it is because of their shorter lives that they strive to achieve as much as they can in the years they are given. Whatever drives them, humans are the innovators, the achievers, and the pioneers of the worlds. With their penchant for migration and conquest, humans are more physically diverse than other common races. There is no typical human. Humans are the most adaptable and ambitious people among the common races. They have widely varying tastes, morals, and customs in the many different lands where they have settled. As a very diverse people, humans are generally more accepting of other races, and are more accepted themselves because of that. Humans speak the Common Tongue plus one more language.

Abilities
Chosen Specialty: Humans are a diverse people who choose their own path. Human characters have two more stat points to spend at Level 1.

Almanac

Check (Rolling a stat check): Used interchangeably with rolling a save, this means to roll the d20 and apply the character's bonus in whatever stat is being checked to see if they pass or fail the event.

Level: An indicator of a character's power. Each successive level grants a player 1 point in each of the 4 main skills along with 2 points the player may allocate as they please.

Primary Action (P): A class of action that takes up a player's entire turn except for movement.

Save (Rolling a save): See Check

Secondary Action (S): A class of action that a player can do up to two of in a turn. A secondary action cannot be done in the same turn as a primary action.

Turn: A player's action phase. It can either consist of up to 2 secondary actions or a single primary action, along with movement which is considered a "free" action.
